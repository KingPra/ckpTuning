Pre-dyno Checklist

Fuel:

  • You should have fresh fuel
  • Make sure you have enought fuel
  • Fuel pump is operating properly
  • Fuel pump is proper size
  • Fuel filter(s) is/are flowing properly
  • Fuel injectors should be sized for your power goals
  • Fuel pressure gauge is recommended
  • Make sure you don't have any fuel leaks

    Mechanical:

  • Check for proper oil level
  • Check for proper coolant level
  • Check for proper compression
  • Check valve lash
  • Timing belt should be within specs
  • Check ignition timing
  • Make sure your clutch is not slipping
  • Check for vacuum leaks

    Electrical:

  • Check all Connections
  • Check for damaged Cables/wires
  • Check grounds
  • Check that alternator is within specs
  • Ignition wires should be OEM quality or better
  • check distributor, cap and rotor/ coil packs for proper operation

    Turbo Systems:

  • Check for shaft play
  • Check oil lines are properly installed
  • Check that all pipes and clamps are torqued to spec
  • Check for leaks
  • Check wastegate spring pressure

    Exhaust:

  • Make sure O2 sensor can be removed (necessary for dyno wideband readout)
  • Exhaust should be properly sized for power output goals
  • Catalytic converters are not recommended for race car (not street legal)

Summary:

This checklist is not definitive, this is just the bare minimum that should be looked at. This checklist will help minimize time and money spent on troubleshooting during your tuning session. If vehicle is not ready during tuning session, we may not be able to complete your tune in one session. Appointments cut short due to vehicle not being properly prepped will result in a loss of deposit and any other fees acquired. Our labor rates apply for time spent troubleshooting. There will be a $50 clean up fee for any leaks on the dyno.
